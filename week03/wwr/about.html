History

White water rafting as a commercial sport emerged in the mid-20th century, evolving from military and exploratory uses of rubber rafts. Companies like those on the Snake River in the 1950s and the Salmon River in the 1940s paved the way for the modern industry. The inclusion of whitewater sports in the Olympics, particularly the 1972 Munich Games, further propelled its popularity and growth.
